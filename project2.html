Dual-Sense Circularly Polarized Antenna With a Dual Coupled Line for Worldwide UHF RFID Applications


ABSTRACT

MICROSTRIP patch has been widely used in designing a circularly polarized (CP) antenna due to its low profile and low cost. By etching an asymmetric slot and loading a coupled strip at each side of a square patch, a CP antenna of wide axial ratio (AR) beamwidth is obtained. The design in utilizes the octagon-star shape to produce two orthogonal degenerated TM11 modes. A circular patch with two notches can also realize the CP operation. Two orthogonal electric fields with a phase difference (PD) of 90° can be easily stimulated by a feeding network.
OBJECTIVE 1: A dual-sense circularly polarized (CP) microstrip patch antenna with a dual- coupled line. The rectangular patch antenna operating in TM01 mode is utilized as a basic design, and a single-coupled line is introduced to excite the TM10 mode with a phase difference of 90°, generating CP operation. As different polarizations can be obtained by changing the position of the single-coupled line, a dual-coupled line is introduced to the rectangular patch antenna to excite two CP modes with different polarization .The prototype of the dual-band dual-sense CP antenna is fabricated and measured.
OBJECTIVE 2: The antenna is composed of two coin-shaped patches, a ring-shaped strip, and a suspended feeding strip with open-circuited termination. . The proposed antenna can be a candidate for universal RFID readers at the UHF band of 840–955MHz.
OBJECTIVE 3: A wideband dual-feed, dual-sense circularly polarized (CP) dielectric resonator antenna (DRA) is proposed and demonstrated. An L-shaped slot and a vertical metal strip, fed by the same microstrip line, simultaneously excite the antenna. Using this feeding method, a dual-band, dual-sense CP triangular DRA is obtained by employing the DRA mode excited at the lower band and the hybrid mode excited at the upper band. The dual-sense dual-band CP antenna with a coupled line can attain good bandwidths with a compact structure.
A wide 3 dB AR beam width and a small frequency ratio of 1.04 is successfully obtained. Also, a frequency and polarization reconfigurable antenna can be easily designed according to the working principle of the coupled line if needed. The microstrip patch antenna with a dual-coupled line will be a strong candidate for a dualsense wireless communication application of a small frequency ratio.
INDEX TERMS: Circular polarized, dual-sense, dual-coupled line, radio frequency identification (RFID), dielectric resonator antenna (DRA), dual-feed, dual-sense, hybrid mode.
